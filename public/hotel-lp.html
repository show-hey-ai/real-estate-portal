Before You Buy
5 risks that catch foreign
hotel buyers in Tokyo
Each one can cost months of delay or significant unexpected costs. Our review is designed to flag these early.
1

Buying a building that can't legally become a hotel

Not every building qualifies. Zoning classification, building use category (youto), and neighborhood agreements all affect hotel license eligibility. This needs to be checked before you sign anything.

2

Underestimating fire code and renovation costs

Fire safety upgrades are the biggest budget surprise for foreign buyers. Sprinkler systems, emergency lighting, evacuation routes — costs vary dramatically by building age and structure.

3

Using fragmented vendors with no coordination

Separate broker, lawyer, architect, contractor, and licensing consultant — with no one coordinating across them. Important details fall through the cracks, especially across language barriers.

4

Assuming visa eligibility is automatic

A Business Manager Visa involves specific requirements around office setup, capital, business structure, and immigration review. Getting the structure wrong early can delay your entire project.

5

Relying on projected yields without local verification

Returns vary widely by location, building condition, licensing path, and operating model. Numbers that look good on paper need to be stress-tested against local realities.

Our review checks for all five — before you commit.
